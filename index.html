The use of chemical substances such as azamethiphos, deltamethrin, teflubenzuron, emamectin and cypermethrin, to mitigate the development of parasites amongst farm-raised salmon populations can pose serious consequences for the marine environment. At high enough concentrations such chemicals can represent a potential hazard to farmed fish, local flora and fauna [1-3] as well as humans, e.g., water ingestion by swimmers [4]. To this extent, regulations exist for the dispersion of such chemicals in the marine environment in the form of environmental quality standards (EQS) and maximum allowable concentrations (MAC) [5].
